I LOVE technology, from playing videogames, watching movies, listening to music, or surfing the web, it’s become an integral facet of my existence. So, it wasn’t too much of a stretch for me to fall in love with software development. From console.log(“hello world”) to ); DROP TABLE students; I’ve been engaged and excited by all of the amazing things I can do with even a little bit of time, energy, and effort.

I enjoy working on design, but don’t have much of an eye for it. I’ve looked into material design and done some reading on color theory, but I believe that ultimately it comes down to je ne sais quoi, and I’ve been meaning to stock up on sais for a while now.

I would argue that front-end development is the most fun because it’s where the cool stuff actually comes into play. You can tell the server(s) what you want and then use that to interact with the user. Getting components to draw and trying to make the application seem like it’s alive to entrance and amaze all who see it!

As much fun as the front-end can be, I think the back-end is a load of CRUD! It’s where the application gains a sense of permanence and really makes it feel like it's an actual entity. I love mapping out the complicated relationships, the whole process of thinking out exactly what’s needed, and who is allowed to access what at which routes!

Ultimately, I love programming because of the challenge it provides me. Engaging my mind in a worthwhile and complex task. You also can’t beat the high of not testing for a while and then having your code actually somehow work on the first try!

Curb-Side

Curb-Side is an application that allows businesses to register and create catalogues that customers are then able to use to create and place orders. Which can then be finished at the businesses curb. I worked with three other teammates, we utilized scrum methodologies and constantly used pair-programming. I was largely involved with writing the server and the "business" UI of the front end. This is my favorite application that I've worked on by far because of all of the cool things we were able to do. We used sockets to make a chat feature, and implemented GoogleApis and Mongoose GeoJSON to allow users to search for businesses by typing in a location.

Bug Report

Bug report is an application that allows users to post bugs, make notes on posted bugs. And the creator of a bug can close it once it has been resolved. The information is stored in an easy to read format that makes obvious who submitted the bug, its status and the last time it was modified which mostly references when it was created. This is one of the earliest full-stack front to back applications I wrote by myself. I had a great deal of fun writing it, and was very proud of the design at the time. Looking back I would try to implement more concepts from material design, but I think this is a good representation of what I was capable of at the time.

Project Manager

Project manager uses a kanban approach to organizing tasks. Users are able to log in, create boards, lists and tasks, and move tasks between lists. I worked with a partner consistently pair-programming, we swapped regularly who was coding and who was driving, so I participated in the entire back and front end. We were a bit heavy handed with the shadows... But I think it's a cool application, and presented some interesting challenges.
